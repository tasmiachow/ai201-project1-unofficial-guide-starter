### Heckscher Playground
West Side between 61st and 63rd

Heckscher Playground is not only the oldest playground in the Park (created in 1927), but it’s also the largest at 1.8 acres—providing plenty of space for kids with lots of energy! This playground includes swings, slides, a maze-like climber, an extensive water feature, and other fun equipment with a stunning view of midtown Manhattan. Its primary entrance is the central breezeway of the Heckscher Building, which houses restrooms—making it a convenient place for families to spend the day.

Heckscher Playground was renovated during the 1960s and 1970s with adventure-style play features designed by Richard Dattner. Adventure-style playgrounds—an alternative to playgrounds with traditional equipment—typically include interconnected play features that encourage exploration and imaginative play. The Park’s other adventure-style playgrounds are the East 72nd Street Playground, Ancient Playground, Tarr Family Playground, Adventure Playground, and Billy Johnson Playground. The Conservancy rebuilt Heckscher Playground in 2006.

Central Park has 21 playgrounds that are unique in design and character. Most of them were built in the 1930s as part of a system of playgrounds located along the Park perimeter. Since 2011—as part of the Plan for Play initiative—the Conservancy has been renovating all the Park’s playgrounds, with the goal of bringing each of them up to the same standard of excellence and making them feel more connected to the Park’s surrounding landscapes.

## Recreation & Cultural Facilities

### --- South End Facilities ---

### Central Park Carousel
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** A classic, historic amusement ride located in the southern part of the park, popular for families and children.

### Heckscher Ballfields
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** A historic cluster of softball fields nestled under mature trees, featuring a striking view of the 59th Street skyline in the background.

### Wollman Rink
* **Location:** South End (Includes Dining)
* **Description:** A massive outdoor rink used for ice skating in the winter months and seasonal activities/dining in the warmer seasons.

### Tisch Children's Zoo
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** A dedicated, family-friendly zoo space tailored specifically for children to interact with animals.

### Volleyball Courts
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** Public outdoor courts designated for recreational and competitive volleyball games.

### Lawn Bowling and Croquet Greens
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** Highly manicured, dedicated grass lawns where local players practice traditional lawn bowling and croquet in the summer.

### Rumsey Playfield
* **Location:** South End
* **Description:** A primary outdoor venue utilized for summer concerts, cultural performances, and major public events.

### Grand Army Plaza
* **Location:** South End (Southeast Entrance)
* **Description:** A formal European-style plaza featuring the iconic golden equestrian statue of General Sherman, serving as a major gateway to the park.

---

### --- Mid-Park Facilities ---

### The Great Lawn
* **Location:** Mid-Park (Landscapes & Recreation)
* **Description:** A sweeping, 55-acre green landscape in the center of the park. It is a premier destination for picnics, sunbathing, and relaxing against a backdrop of the 57th Street skyline.

### Central Park Boathouse
* **Location:** Mid-Park (Includes Dining)
* **Description:** An iconic lakeside facility where visitors can rent rowboats, view the water, or dine right along the edge of The Lake.

### Kerbs Boathouse
* **Location:** Mid-Park (Conservatory Water)
* **Description:** A charming facility reflected in the waters of the Conservatory Water, famous for model sailboat racing and a nearby cafe.

### Delacorte Theater
* **Location:** Mid-Park
* **Description:** An open-air amphitheater nestled inside the park, famously home to the annual Shakespeare in the Park summer theater productions.

### Naumburg Bandshell
* **Location:** Mid-Park (The Mall)
* **Description:** A historic stone architectural features bandshell situated on the Mall, hosting concerts and public performances on beautiful summer days.

### Swedish Cottage (Marionette Theatre)
* **Location:** Mid-Park (Architectural Features)
* **Description:** A traditional imported Swedish wooden cottage that serves as a unique marionette theater hosting whimsical plays.

### Tennis Courts

* **Description:** Large public tennis complexes available for recreational play and permit-holders in the northern section of the park.

### North Meadow
* **Location:** North End (Landscapes & Recreation)

### North Meadow Center

### Stephanie and Fred Shuman Running Track
