I've ran both Prospect Park and Central Park in equal measure. Here's my take:

Both of the parks have similar characteristics terrain. Undulating loops with some tough hills of their own – but nothing crazy like Harlem Hills (Central Park). Prospect Park's Battle Pass Hill can be a challenge if you don't anticipate it. Central Park will have the highest elevation gain compared to Prospect Park.

Both parks are equally shaded. I don't find myself feeling like I'm exposed for too long in Prospect Park.

Key thing about Prospect Park, as others have mentioned, it doesn't get as congested as Central Park. Minimal tourists. No horse carriages or poo to worry about. It's a more chill environment for runners, especially if you run the early evening hours.

Prospect Park was two running lanes but most folks run on the left lane.

Neither parks are better than the other for running. I enjoy running through them both for their own characteristics and challenges.


Both are great for running. Really amazing when you consider we’re in a major city.

Central Park has more run options probably —bridal path, reservoir, hidden track in north west corner, good half mile paved loop by the Delcorte theatre, ability to do 6 mile loop, 1.7 mile lower loop, 4 mile loop, etc. without really thinking about it. But the tourists. Bike cab things. Horses. Tourists. Tourists. Tourists. Tourists. But it has loads of run clubs and that’s cool.

Prospect park has a wonderful 3.3 mile loop or running outside on the sidewalks for 4 miles for variety. But the shorter loop makes long runs a bit more mind numbing. IMO Prospect is better for trail runs, you have to know it a bit to get your mileage in the trees but it’s a nice change from pavement. It has great run clubs though not as many; more low key 5K and 10ks going like every weekend. Has annoying strollers. But way more tolerable than tourists. Lately has a lot of those moped douches. Way worse than cyclists. Central Park seems to have less of that.

Both parks are equally shaded. Level of cleanliness is comparable. Both have great transverses - perfect for shorter repeats. Both have good short hills for speedwork (though the garbage hill in Central Park stinks but is excellent for short hill repeats).

Someone dinged the cyclists in both parks. Whatever. I never have issue with them when I’m running. Most people use the run lane to run. Bike lane to bike. Obviously some ignore either, but that’s with everything in life. Prospect park is significantly a better park to ride any kind of bike in though (see: no tourists; Do watch out for strollers).
